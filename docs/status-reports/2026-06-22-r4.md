## Challenge findings (before building)

The kickoff recommended **calling `GET /analysis/net-worth`** and reading its current totals "so the two
surfaces never drift." I challenged this against the actual SQL and **chose the client-side calc instead**:

- **The two surfaces cannot drift, because the classifier — not the data source — was the real risk.**
  `v_account_balances` ([`migrate.ts`](../../apps/api/src/db/migrate.ts)) sums **every** transaction per
  account with no archived/kind filter; `R9`'s net-worth query
  ([`analysisService.ts`](../../apps/api/src/services/analysisService.ts)) sums the **same** all-transactions
  set, grouped by `account.kind`. So `Σ(account.balanceCents)` grouped by `isLiabilityKind` over the full
  account list is **provably equal** to `R9`'s headline totals. The drift the kickoff worried about was the
  roadmap's sketched **sign-based** rule; **reusing `isLiabilityKind`** (not re-deriving a sign split)
  neutralizes it entirely.
- **Client-side is the better fit for this surface.** The Dashboard already loads the account list, so the
  snapshot renders instantly with **no 3rd fetch** on the daily-use hot path and **no new loading/error
  state**. It is also a **faithful `Σ` of the per-account balances rendered right above it** — calling the
  endpoint would place endpoint-derived totals next to view-derived rows, a weaker local-consistency story.
- **The one decision client-side forces me to own: archived accounts.** `R9` includes them (no filter), and
  the stated goal is agreement with `NetWorthView` — so the snapshot sums **all** accounts (active +
  archived), not just the visible active rows. An archived account still holds its money/debt.

**Decision:** a pure front-end derived display — `NetWorthSummary` in `Dashboard.tsx`, reusing
`isLiabilityKind`, summing all accounts. No new endpoint, no schema change, no new domain primitive.

## What R4 built

### `NetWorthSummary` (pure front-end, in `Dashboard.tsx`)

A small presentational component rendered inside the accounts `<section>`, below `<AccountList>`. It folds
the already-loaded `accounts` into `assetsCents` / `liabilitiesCents` (split by `isLiabilityKind(a.kind)`)
and `netCents = assets + liabilities`, then renders a `<table>` (caption "Net worth summary"; rows with
`<th scope="row">` — the same accessible shape as the `NetWorthView` "Current totals" table). Hidden until
at least one account exists (`accounts === null || length === 0` → renders nothing). Liabilities display
negative (e.g. `-$300.00`), exactly as in `NetWorthView`.
